## 1. Research Novelty

### Evaluation Criteria
- **Originality**: Is this the first study addressing this specific question?
- **Innovation Level**: Does the work introduce new concepts, methods, or insights?
- **Significance**: Will this contribution advance the field?

### Key Questions
- What is the specific advance beyond previous work?
- Are the research questions clearly defined and justified?
- Does the study fill a gap in current knowledge?

### Common Issues
- Incremental work presented as groundbreaking
- Claims of novelty not supported by comparison with existing literature
- Similar work already published without acknowledgment

## 2. Theoretical Framework

### Evaluation Criteria
- **Clarity**: Is the theoretical basis clearly explained?
- **Completeness**: Are all necessary concepts defined?
- **Appropriateness**: Is the theoretical framework suitable for the research?

### Key Questions
- Is the theoretical background sufficient for understanding the study?
- Are key terms and concepts clearly defined?
- Does the theory align with the methodology?

### Common Issues
- Insufficient theoretical justification
- Missing definitions of key variables
- Mismatch between theory and empirical approach

---

## 3. Methodology

### Evaluation Criteria
- **Appropriateness**: Are the methods suitable for the research questions?
- **Technical Soundness**: Are the procedures correct and well-executed?
- **Reproducibility**: Can another researcher replicate the study?

### Key Questions
- Are the methods clearly described?
- Is the sample size/condition appropriate?
- Are statistical methods properly selected and reported?

### Common Issues
- Methods section incomplete or vague
- Inappropriate statistical tests
- Lack of detail for replication

---

## 4. Experimental Design

### Evaluation Criteria
- **Structure**: Is the experimental design logical and systematic?
- **Controls**: Are proper controls included?
- **Variables**: Are independent and dependent variables identified?

### Key Questions
- Does the design address the research questions?
- Are there confounding variables?
- Is the design adequately powered?

### Common Issues
- Missing control groups
- Inadequate randomization
- Confounding variables not addressed

---

## 5. Data Analysis

### Evaluation Criteria
- **Accuracy**: Are the data correctly processed and analyzed?
- **Transparency**: Are analysis steps clearly documented?
- **Appropriateness**: Are suitable statistical methods used?

### Key Questions
- Are raw data accessible if needed?
- Are assumptions of statistical tests verified?
- Are effect sizes and confidence intervals reported?

### Common Issues
- Incorrect statistical procedures
- Violation of statistical assumptions
- Selective reporting of results

---

## 6. Results Interpretation

### Evaluation Criteria
- **Objectivity**: Are findings presented without bias?
- **Integration**: Are results connected to research questions?
- **Causality Claims**: Are causal inferences appropriately limited?

### Key Questions
- Do interpretations align with the data?
- Are alternative explanations considered?
- Are limitations acknowledged?

### Common Issues
- Overinterpretation of results
- Claims not supported by data
- Limitations insufficiently discussed

---

## 7. Writing Quality

### Evaluation Criteria
- **Clarity**: Is the text clear and understandable?
- **Organization**: Is the paper well-structured?
- **Language**: Is the English appropriate for publication?

### Key Questions
- Is the manuscript well-organized with logical flow?
- Are technical terms used correctly and consistently?
- Is the length appropriate for the content?

### Common Issues
- Poor organization and flow
- Inconsistent terminology
- Grammar and spelling errors

---

## 8. Figures & Tables

### Evaluation Criteria
- **Quality**: Are figures clear and well-labeled?
- **Information**: Do they effectively convey data?
- **Integration**: Are figures properly referenced in text?

### Key Questions
- Are all figures and tables necessary?
- Are axes properly labeled with units?
- Is statistical information provided where appropriate?

### Common Issues
- Poor figure quality or resolution
- Missing or incomplete legends
- Redundant presentation of data

---

## 9. Literature References

### Evaluation Criteria
- **Relevance**: Are key references included?
- **Currency**: Is the literature up-to-date?
- **Accuracy**: Are citations correct and complete?

### Key Questions
- Are important foundational works cited?
- Is recent literature adequately covered?
- Are all citations traceable and accurate?

### Common Issues
- Missing important citations
- Outdated references
- Citation format inconsistencies

---

## Severity Classification Guide

### Fatal Issues
- Scientific errors that invalidate conclusions
- Fabricated or falsified data
- Plagiarism or severe ethical violations

### Major Issues
- Significant methodological flaws
- Major gaps in logic or evidence
- Critical missing controls or data

### Minor Issues
- Small errors that don't affect conclusions
- Suggestions for improvement
- Minor clarity or presentation issues

### Editorial Issues
- Formatting inconsistencies
- Language improvements needed
- Reference style issues
